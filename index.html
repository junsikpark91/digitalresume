The Virtual Construction Worksites

  The construction industry is one of the most accident-prone industries in the United States. Indeed, many accidents occur on scaffolds, making it necessary to investigate the cause of these accidents. Many safety studies focusing on the construction industry have investigated the efficiency of training tools for workers in hazardous situations. However, there have been few investigations of how workers’ perceptions are related to construction site accidents. To help fill this gap, this study examined the visual stimuli that influence workers’ perceptions.

The goal of the study was to investigate the effect of visual acuity, brightness, and color in terms of size estimation error in the virtual environment.

The scaffold sizes (height, length, width) were estimated in the virtual environment using a head-mounted display (HTC-VIVE). The experiment conditions were varied in visual acuity (high, low), brightness (high, low), and color (red, green, blue, gray). Mental workload and virtual sickness levels were collected through the questionnaire.

The visual acuity effect found on height estimation. Higher height estimation error showed in high visual acuity level than a low visual acuity level. Also, mental workload measures were significantly correlated with height size estimation error.

Heart Disease Prediction

Heart disease is one of the most prevalent diseases in the US. Many studies explored predictors of this disease. The goal of the project was to build a robust model of predicting heart disease with available data.

The data was from UCI machine learning repository (Aha and Kiebler.,1988) which has 13 attributes without missing data with 303 entities.

Three different logit models were created and compared based on attributes related to Framingham Heart Study, Cleveland Study, and mixed model.

A stochastic logistic regression model using gradient ascend method was developed to predict the existence of heart disease, coronary artery disease (CAD). The model’s best accuracy was 92% and averaged 82%. Cross-validation was used to show the robustness of the model.
